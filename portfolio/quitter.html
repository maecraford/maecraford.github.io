The Quitter

The Quitter is a poem written in 1912 by Robert Service

It was read to me when I was young by my Grandfather, along with many other of Robert Service's poems.

I came back to this poem in mid 2020 and it became the center of a few projects I worked on throughout my fall semester.

The Quitter


When you're lost in the Wild, and you're scared as a child,
And Death looks you bang in the eye,
And you're sore as a boil, it's according to Hoyle
To cock your revolver and . . . die.
But the Code of a Man says: "Fight all you can,"
And self-dissolution is barred.
In hunger and woe, oh, it's easy to blow . . .
It's the hell-served-for-breakfast that's hard.


"You're sick of the game!" Well, now, that's a shame.
You're young and you're brave and you're bright.
"You've had a raw deal!" I know — but don't squeal,
Buck up, do your damnedest, and fight.
It's the plugging away that will win you the day,
So don't be a piker, old pard!
Just draw on your grit; it's so easy to quit:
It's the keeping-your-chin-up that's hard.


It's easy to cry that you're beaten — and die;
It's easy to crawfish and crawl;
But to fight and to fight when hope's out of sight —
Why, that's the best game of them all!
And though you come out of each gruelling bout,
All broken and beaten and scarred,
Just have one more try — it's dead easy to die,
It's the keeping-on-living that's hard.

The first project I made based on this poem was a poster. I chose to fill the background with a screenshot of Monty Python and The Holy Grail.

The second project I worked on using the poem was a website.

As you read the poem and scroll over each line either a picture, gif, or link pops up to add more definition to each line.

If you would like to explore the website for yourself you can right here

The third project I based the poem on was an experimental video.

In order to make the video I interpreted certain lines of the poem into websites and daily tasks which I accomplish on my computer. I then screen recorded myself going throuhg the collection of pages I had collected.

I set the whole video to the openeing of the song Johnny Bravo by Bobbing.

Special thanks to All The President's Men for the opening scene.

The final project I used the poem for was a collection of postcards.

All four are actual postcards I collaged with collected pieces of paper. I printed out the poem and attatched a verse to each card.
